Subthesis 1: Grief and Memory

The first subthesis evidence from Suzume comes from the important scene at 1:35:00, when Suzume returns to the ruins of her childhood home and enters the Ever After. There, she says to her younger self:

“You must have been so lonely... I’m sorry I left you behind. But it’s okay now. You’re not alone anymore. I’m here.”

In this moment, Suzume goes back to the place where she experienced her biggest loss, the place where she lost her mother, and meets her younger self. This moment is symbolic and shows how she finally faces the sadness she has been hiding for many years. By hugging and speaking to her younger self, Suzume starts to accept what happened. She understands that the pain of losing her mother will always be there, but it doesn't have to control her life. The dreamlike world of the Ever After, with its broken buildings and soft light, helps show how fragile and emotional this memory is (Meng et al.; Fitaloka et al.).

This powerful scene shows what many have described as a strong symbol of healing and self-acceptance (The Conversation). It marks a key point in Suzume’s journey where she begins to let go of the pain and move forward. Her return to the ruins and the way she connects with her past show how anime can use fantasy and visuals to express deep emotions. This moment supports the main thesis by showing an important step in Suzume’s emotional growth.
