Symbolism and Emotion in Suzume
Ryan Lee

Welcome to My Anime Project

Research Question: What have you learned about Japanese culture from the work of your choice?

For my Traditional/Written Essay Project, I will be analyzing the anime Suzume. The director Makoto Shinkai was born on February 9, 1973, and the anime was released in 2022, and distributed by Toho Co., Ltd. This individual project will focus on exploring the film’s themes, visual style, and emotional impact, particularly in relation to grief, loss, and healing.

My thesis statement is that in Suzume, the journey of grief and healing is portrayed through symbolic encounters with the past and meaningful objects to explore the emotional journey of grief that ultimately leads to healing. To elaborate more on my thesis statement the premise of the anime Suzume is about a teenage girl named Suzume who lost her mother due to the 2011 Tohoku earthquake at a young age and later meets a man called Souta and they go on a journey together to save the world from a disaster. However, along this journey, many events happen, and it is unclear what Suzume’s relationship with some of these characters are. Suzume grows emotionally and is able to accept the passing of her mother through comforting her younger self. I want to focus on aspects of the anime that connect emotionally with the audience like the artwork that were all chosen carefully to add more depth to the scenes.
